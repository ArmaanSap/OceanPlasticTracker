How can I help?

What is ocean plastic pollution?

Every year, millions of tons of plastic enter the ocean. It breaks down into microplastics that enter the food chain and persist for hundreds of years. The Atlantic alone contains several major garbage patches where currents trap debris in slow-moving gyres.

How to reduce plastic waste

Use reusable bags, bottles, and containers. Avoid single-use plastics. Participate in local beach or river cleanups. Support businesses that use sustainable packaging. Properly dispose of and recycle plastics so they don't enter waterways.

How this simulator works

This tool uses real NOAA ocean current data to simulate how a piece of trash drifts over time. Choose a starting location, trash type, and duration to see where it ends up, and how it affects the ecosystem along the way. The path is calculated using bilinear interpolation on a 0.2° resolution current grid updated daily.

Building Awareness

Trash in the ocean is a real and pressing issue, but by making the invisible visible, we hope to inspire action and awareness.
